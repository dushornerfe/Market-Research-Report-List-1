<p><p>Electrocoating, or E-Coat, is a painting process that uses electrical currents to deposit paint onto metal surfaces. The market is primarily divided into two types: cathodic and anodic. Cathodic electrocoating uses negative electrodes to attract positively charged paint particles, resulting in a thicker, durable coating ideal for corrosion resistance. Anodic electrocoating employs positive electrodes, providing a smooth finish but is generally less resistant to corrosion. Both methods cater to diverse industrial applications, ensuring effective surface protection and aesthetic appeal.</p></p>
