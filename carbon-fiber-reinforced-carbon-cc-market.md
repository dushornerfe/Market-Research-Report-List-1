<p><p>Carbon Fiber-Reinforced Carbon (C/C) is a composite material known for its high thermal stability and lightweight characteristics, widely used in aerospace and automotive applications. The market is categorized by production methods like Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and Liquid Impregnation Process. CVD involves the deposition of carbon onto a substrate through gas-phase reactions, producing dense, high-performance materials. In contrast, the Liquid Impregnation Process entails soaking carbon fibers in a resin, followed by curing, resulting in a robust composite suitable for various industrial applications.</p></p>
