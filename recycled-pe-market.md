<p><p>The recycled polyethylene (PE) market primarily consists of two types: recycled low-density polyethylene (LDPE) and recycled high-density polyethylene (HDPE). Recycled LDPE is commonly sourced from flexible packaging, such as plastic wraps and bags, and is often used in products like trash liners and floor tiles. In contrast, recycled HDPE, derived from rigid containers like bottles and jugs, is favored for its durability and is used in applications such as piping, automotive parts, and construction materials. Both segments contribute to sustainable practices by reducing plastic waste.</p></p>
