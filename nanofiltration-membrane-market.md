<p><p>Nanofiltration membranes are categorized into three main types: polymeric, inorganic, and hybrid. Polymeric membranes, primarily made of organic polymers, are lightweight and flexible, making them ideal for various applications, including water treatment. Inorganic membranes, constructed from materials like ceramics or metal oxides, offer high thermal and chemical stability, suitable for harsh conditions. Hybrid membranes combine both polymeric and inorganic materials, leveraging the advantages of each type for enhanced performance, durability, and efficiency in separating contaminants from water and other solutions.</p></p>
